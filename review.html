Background

Glasgow Caledonian University (GCU) was founded in 1993 following the merger of Glasgow Polytechnic and Queen's College. It is one of only 29 universities which Ashoka U has been certified as a 'Changemaker Campus.' for promoting higher education as a vehicle for social change. More than 20,000 students study GCU from over 100 nations, making it one of Scotland 's largest universities. Its main campus in Glasgow's city centre is considered to be one of the most modern campuses in the UK. The university is made up of three schools: the School of Engineering and Built Environment, Glasgow School for Business and Society, and the School of Health and Life Sciences. GCU is the only university to offer an Honours degree in Risk Management. ()


The Computing department at Glasgow Caledonian University (GCU) is currently involved in running programmes at three sites: the main GCU campus in Glasgow, African Leadership College (ALC) in Mauritius and National University of Science and Technology (NUST) in Oman.
